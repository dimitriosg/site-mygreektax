**Short answer:** To get an AFM in Greece as a foreigner, visit your local tax office (DOY) with your passport and proof of address — or appoint a representative to do it for you. EU citizens can apply in person; non-EU citizens typically need a legal representative. The process takes one to three working days and the number is issued on the spot in most cases.

The AFM is the first thing almost every expat in Greece needs to sort out, and yet it manages to trip people up constantly. The Greek tax authority website is entirely in Greek, the local tax offices (DOY) have unpredictable hours, and the official guidance assumes you already know how the Greek administrative system works.

This guide covers everything you need to know in 2026: what the AFM actually is, who is required to get one, what documents you need, the three different ways to apply, and what to do once you have your number.

## 1. What is an AFM and why do you need one?

The AFM (Arithmos Forologikou Mitroou, written as AFM or often as ΑΦΜ in Greek) is Greece's Tax Identification Number. It is a unique 9-digit number assigned by the tax authority AADE and tied to you permanently. Think of it as the Greek equivalent of a National Insurance number in the UK or a Social Security Number in the US, but specifically for tax purposes.

Once you have an AFM, it appears on practically every official document you will ever sign in Greece. Landlords need it to register your lease. Banks need it to open an account. Employers need it to put you on payroll. The notary needs it for a property purchase. Even getting a SIM card contract at some providers requires one.

**Important:** Having an AFM does not make you a tax resident of Greece, does not create any immediate tax liability, and does not require you to file a tax return. It is purely a reference number. Tax residency is determined separately, based on how many days you spend in Greece and other factors.

## 2. Who needs an AFM in Greece?

You need an AFM if you plan to do any of the following in Greece:

- Rent or buy property
- Open a bank account
- Work as an employee or freelancer
- Register a business (sole trader, IKE, etc.)
- Apply for a residence permit (Digital Nomad Visa, Golden Visa, etc.)
- Register a vehicle
- Connect utilities in your name
- File a tax return
- Receive any income from Greek sources

Crucially, you do not need to be a resident of Greece to get an AFM. Non-residents who own property or need to conduct any transaction in Greece can and should obtain one. The AFM is issued to both individuals and businesses.

## 3. Documents required

What you need depends on whether you are an EU citizen or a non-EU (third-country) national.

### EU citizens

- Valid passport or national identity card
- Proof of address in Greece or your home country (utility bill, bank statement, or rental contract, typically no older than three months)
- If applying through a representative: a notarised power of attorney

### Non-EU citizens

- Valid passport
- Visa or residence permit (if applicable to your situation)
- Proof of address
- If applying through a representative: a notarised power of attorney, often with an apostille

**Proof of address in Greece when you have just arrived:** If you are renting, an accommodation letter (Υπεύθυνη Δήλωση) from your landlord confirming you are staying at their property is accepted. If staying in a hotel short-term, a hotel booking confirmation has been accepted in some DOY offices, though practice can vary.

## 4. The three ways to get an AFM in Greece

There is no single official "method." In practice, most expats use one of the three approaches below, each with its own tradeoffs.

### Option A: In person at a local tax office (DOY)

The DOY (Dimosia Oikonomiki Ypiresia) is the local tax office. You attend in person, present your documents, and an official processes your application on the spot or within a few days.

- Cost: free
- Timeline: same day to a few working days
- Practical reality: offices are open from around 7:30am to 2:30pm, appointments are recommended, staff rarely speak English, and you need to go to the DOY that corresponds to your area of residence or, for non-residents, the DOY of Foreign Residents in Athens

This works well if you are already in Greece, speak some Greek or have a Greek-speaking companion, and are comfortable navigating Greek bureaucracy.

### Option B: Remote via myAADElive (video verification)

AADE offers a video call verification service called myAADElive, where you connect with a tax authority agent via video, show your documents on camera, and complete the process without visiting an office.

- Cost: free
- Timeline: a few working days after the call
- Language: calls are in Greek; having a Greek speaker present helps significantly
- Availability: appointment slots can be limited; booking in advance is advisable

This is useful if you are outside Greece or cannot easily travel to a tax office. The process still requires Greek-language navigation to book and complete the appointment.

### Option C: Through a licensed Greek accountant or representative

A licensed Greek accountant with a power of attorney from you can attend the DOY on your behalf and handle the entire process. You never need to set foot in a tax office.

- Cost: a service fee applies (at MyGreekTax, AFM registration starts from €39)
- Timeline: typically 24 to 48 hours once documents are in order
- Language: all communication with you is in English; everything with the authorities is handled by the accountant

This is the most efficient option for most expats, especially those who are not yet in Greece, those who have tried the DOY route and found it difficult, or those who simply want the process handled correctly the first time without stress.

## 5. Timeline and costs

The actual issuance of the AFM number is fast once all documents are in order. Delays almost always come from one of three sources: incomplete or unaccepted proof of address, power of attorney that lacks an apostille when one is required, or DOY backlogs during peak periods (typically late spring and summer when the tourist season brings more non-resident applications).

If you are applying through a representative and have all documents ready, 24 to 48 hours is a realistic expectation for a straightforward case.

## 6. What to do after you get your AFM

Getting the number itself is only the first step. Most expats living in Greece, or planning to, will also need to activate their TAXISnet account (the Greek tax authority's online portal) and set up myAADE access. This unlocks the ability to file tax returns, accept rental contracts digitally, and interact with the tax authority online.

The full activation process involves obtaining a kleidarithmos (authentication key, a multi-digit code issued by the DOY), then using it to activate your TAXISnet credentials. It sounds simple and usually is, but the process has changed several times in recent years and the AADE website is only partially translated into English.

**AFM Basic vs AFM Plus Setup:** If you only need the 9-digit number for a single transaction (say, signing a property purchase) and will not be living in Greece or filing returns, you need AFM Basic only. If you are moving to Greece, working here, or will need to interact with the tax authority online at any point, you need the full setup including TAXISnet activation and myAADE access.

If you are moving to Greece and becoming a tax resident, there is a further step: formally declaring your tax residency change. This is a separate process from obtaining an AFM and carries significant implications for your worldwide income obligations. It is covered in detail in our guide to [the 183-day rule and Greek tax residency](/blog/greek-tax-resident-183-day-rule/).

## 7. Common mistakes to avoid

- **Using an old or unofficial address document.** Many DOY offices are strict about the three-month rule for proof of address. An old lease or a bank statement from six months ago will typically be rejected.
- **Confusing AFM registration with tax residency registration.** Getting an AFM does not tell the Greek tax authority that you live in Greece or that you are a tax resident. If you are moving to Greece and spending more than 183 days a year there, you need to take the additional step of formally transferring your tax residency.
- **Skipping TAXISnet activation.** If you receive a Greek pension, rental income, or any income that needs to be declared in Greece, you will eventually need a TAXISnet account. It is much easier to set this up at the same time as the AFM rather than separately later.
- **Using an unregulated service.** Only a licensed Greek accountant registered with the OEE (Economic Chamber of Greece) can legally represent you in dealings with the tax authority. Check that whoever you use is properly registered.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is an AFM in Greece?**
The AFM (Arithmos Forologikou Mitroou) is your Greek Tax Identification Number, a unique 9-digit number issued by the tax authority AADE. It is required for almost every financial or legal transaction in Greece, including renting or buying property, opening a bank account, signing an employment contract, registering a vehicle, and filing tax returns.

**Do I need an AFM if I am not a Greek resident?**
Yes. You can obtain an AFM even if you are not a tax resident in Greece and do not live there permanently. Non-residents who own property, need to sign a contract, or conduct any financial activity in Greece are required to have one.

**What documents do I need to get an AFM in Greece?**
For EU citizens: a valid passport or national identity card, plus proof of address. For non-EU citizens: a valid passport, visa or residence permit if applicable, and proof of address. If applying through a representative, a notarised power of attorney is required.

**How long does it take to get an AFM?**
At a local tax office in person: the same day or within a few working days. Through a licensed representative with full documents: typically 24 to 48 hours. Via myAADElive video call: usually a few working days after the call.

**Can I get an AFM without visiting Greece?**
Yes. Either via the myAADElive video verification service, where you present documents on a video call with a tax authority agent, or by appointing a licensed Greek accountant or representative with a notarised power of attorney. The second option is the most straightforward for non-Greek speakers.

**Does getting an AFM make me a tax resident of Greece?**
No. An AFM is simply a reference number. Having one does not make you a Greek tax resident, does not create any tax liability, and does not require you to file a tax return. Tax residency is determined separately based on factors like how many days you spend in Greece each year. If you are moving to Greece and will spend more than 183 days per year there, you need to formally register as a tax resident, which is a separate process.

**What is the difference between AFM Basic and AFM Plus Setup?**
AFM Basic means you get the 9-digit number only. AFM Plus Setup adds TAXISnet activation, myAADE account creation, and kleidarithmos, everything you need to use Greek digital government services. If you are moving to Greece or plan to file taxes at any point, you need the full setup.
